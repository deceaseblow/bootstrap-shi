Frequently Asked Questions

Players control a unique character, known as a "Champion," with its own abilities and playstyle. Two teams of five players each battle on a map called Summoner's Rift. The goal is to destroy the enemy's Nexus while defending your own. Players work together to kill enemy minions, monsters, and champions to gain gold, experience, and objectives to help push forward.

You can unlock new champions by earning Blue Essence through gameplay, completing missions, or purchasing them with Riot Points, which are acquired with real money.

Top Lane: Often features tanky or bruiser champions. Jungle: A player who roams the jungle to kill neutral monsters and assist teammates. Mid Lane: Often a mage or assassin champion, responsible for controlling the center of the map. Bot Lane (ADC & Support): ADC (Attack Damage Carry) deals the damage, while the Support helps the ADC and provides utility for the team.

In Normal mode, players can play casually without affecting their rank, while in Ranked mode, players compete to climb the ranked ladder. Winning games in Ranked increases your rank, while losing can decrease it.
